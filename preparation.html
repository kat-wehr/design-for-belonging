Designing learning experiences, materials, and systems with belonging in mind before students arrive

What is it?

Before course launch, we are preparing. From a belonging lens, preparation is the time to inventory all the tools, strategies, resources, and pedagogical approaches that appear in the course. If this is a new course, this is the time where we collaborate with colleagues and model belonging behaviors in our work together. For existing courses, this moment is an opportunity to evaluate the alignment among all the existing structures and practices in the course and reflect on whether they support or conflict with belonging goals.

Why It Matters for Belonging

The preparation phase sets the stage for cultivating belonging throughout the learning experience. During preparation, changes can be made to better align the tools and practices students will experience to ensure all of these work in harmony to support belonging.

Practice Strategies

  • Inventory and critically evaluate any technology tools used in the course. Check out the 5 Critical Questions Framework (linked below) from the folks at Civis of Technology for a starting point!
  • Review and incorporate accessibility and inclusive design resources like UDL
  • Reflect on what faculty can do to support belonging, and help them make a plan to act
