Step 2: Understanding Belonging in Learning

Belonging in educational contexts means students feel valued, included, and able to be their authentic selves while learning. Research shows it directly impacts engagement, retention, and success.

Key Concepts to Remember:

  • Belonging is both psychological (feeling valued) and practical (having what you need to succeed)
  • It's especially critical in online and hybrid learning environments
  • Small, intentional actions can make a big difference
  • Belonging benefits ALL students, not just those from underrepresented groups
  • It requires ongoing attention throughout the learning experience
Quick Tip: Think about a time when you felt like you truly belonged somewhere. What specific actions or words made you feel that way? Those same principles apply to your students.

Step 3: Assess Your Current Practices

Before making changes, let's identify what you're already doing well and where there's room for growth.

Belonging Self-Assessment:

  • I use welcoming, inclusive language in my course materials
  • I provide clear expectations and navigation support
  • I acknowledge different learning styles and backgrounds
  • I give timely, constructive feedback that shows I care about student growth
  • I create opportunities for students to connect with each other
  • I check in on student progress and wellbeing regularly
  • I celebrate student achievements and growth
  • I end courses/modules in a way that maintains connections
Reflection: Don't worry if you're not checking all these boxes yet. This assessment helps you prioritize where to focus your belonging-building efforts.
